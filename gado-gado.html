Gado-gado

Description:

Gado-gado is a traditional Indonesian salad that consists of a mix of boiled vegetables, tofu, tempeh, and hard-boiled eggs, all served with a rich peanut sauce dressing. It is a healthy and flavorful dish that is popular throughout Indonesia.

Ingredients:

  • A couple of hard-boiled eggs
  • 2 cups of mixed veggies (like cabbage, green beans, and bean sprouts)
  • 1 large potato, peeled and cubed
  • 1 block of firm tofu, cut into cubes
  • For the peanut sauce:
    • 1/2 cup of creamy peanut butter
    • 2 tbsp of soy sauce
    • 1 tbsp of lime juice
    • a splash of water
    • a pinch of red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. Place the eggs in a saucepan, cover with water, bring to a boil over high heat, and boil for 10 minutes.
  2. Remove the eggs, cool them in ice water for 5 minutes, peel, and slice into halves.
  3. Steam the mixed veggies and potato cubes in a steamer basket over boiling water for 8-10 minutes until tender but still crisp.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat, add the tofu cubes, and pan-fry for 4-5 minutes per side until golden brown.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, water, and red pepper flakes until smooth.
  6. Tip: If the sauce is too thick, add another splash of water and whisk again—it should be pourable but not runny.
  7. Arrange the steamed veggies, potato, tofu, and egg halves on a serving plate.
  8. Drizzle the peanut sauce generously over the top.
  9. Tip: For extra flavor, let the sauce sit for 5 minutes to allow the spices to meld before serving.
  10. Tip: Serve immediately to keep the veggies crisp and avoid sogginess.
