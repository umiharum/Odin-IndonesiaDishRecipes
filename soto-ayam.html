Soto Ayam

Description:

Soto Ayam is a traditional Indonesian chicken soup that is known for its clear, flavorful broth and aromatic spices. It is often served with rice, hard-boiled eggs, and various toppings like bean sprouts and fried wontons.

Ingredients:

  • A couple of chicken thighs, bone-in for extra flavor
  • 4 cups of water
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • A thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced
  • 1 stalk of lemongrass, bruised
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • A pinch of turmeric powder
  • A splash of fish sauce
  • Salt to season
  • A handful of rice noodles
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • A lime, cut into wedges

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat until it shimmers.
  2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, sautéing for about 3 minutes until fragrant and lightly golden. Tip: Don't rush this step—it builds a great flavor base!
  3. Toss in the sliced ginger and bruised lemongrass, stirring for another minute to release their aromas.
  4. Place the chicken thighs into the pot and pour in 4 cups of water, ensuring everything is submerged.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for 25 minutes until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  6. Remove the chicken from the broth and set it aside to cool slightly; once cool enough to handle, shred the meat with two forks, discarding the bones.
  7. Skim any excess fat from the broth surface for a clearer soup. Tip: A fine mesh strainer works wonders here for a cleaner look.
  8. Stir in a pinch of turmeric powder and a splash of fish sauce, then season with salt to your preference.
  9. In a separate pot, cook the rice noodles according to package instructions, usually in boiling water for 5-7 minutes until soft, then drain.
  10. Divide the cooked noodles into bowls, top with shredded chicken, and ladle the hot broth over everything.
    Tip: Garnish with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime right before serving to brighten it up.
